<div class="question-description__3U1T" style="padding-top: 10px;"><div><p>Given <i>n</i> points in the plane that are all pairwise distinct, a "boomerang" is a tuple of points <code>(i, j, k)</code> such that the distance between <code>i</code> and <code>j</code> equals the distance between <code>i</code> and <code>k</code> (<b>the order of the tuple matters</b>).</p>

<p>Find the number of boomerangs. You may assume that <i>n</i> will be at most <b>500</b> and coordinates of points are all in the range <b>[-10000, 10000]</b> (inclusive).</p>

<p><b>Example:</b><br/>
</p><pre><b>Input:</b>
[[0,0],[1,0],[2,0]]

<b>Output:</b>
2

<b>Explanation:</b>
The two boomerangs are <b>[[1,0],[0,0],[2,0]]</b> and <b>[[1,0],[2,0],[0,0]]</b>
</pre>
